into the mental life of the period at which they were written; not by the hasty perusal of
Histories of Philosophy. No student of the Contra Gentiles is likely to acquiesce in the
statement, that the Middle Ages were a time when mankind seemed to have lost the
power of thinking for themselves. Mediaeval people thought for themselves, thoughts
curiously different from ours, and profitable for us to study.
Lastly, the Summa contra Gentiles is mega tekmerion -- considering the ravages of six
and a half centuries of time upon what was once the most harmonious blending of faith
with the science of the day, -- it is a fact of solemn admonition to all Doctors and
Professors of Philosophy and Theology within the Church of Christ, that they should be
at least as solicitous as an English Dean and Chapter now are, for the keeping in yearly
repair of the great edifice given over to their custody; that they should regard with
watchful and intelligent eyes the advance of history, anthropology, criticism and physical
science; and that in their own special sciences they should welcome, and make every
sane endeavour to promote, what since 1845 has been known as the Development of
Doctrine.
